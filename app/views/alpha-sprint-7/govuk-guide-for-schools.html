Convert to an academy: guide for schools

From:
Department for Education
Published
26 November 2015
Updated:
31 January 2025 - See all updates

Applies to England

Summary

How to transfer an academy from one academy trust to another.

If you are the chair of trustees of a single or multi-academy trust (SAT or MAT), you must complete the following steps.

Contents

  • 1. Do due diligence

    Research other academy trusts to understand if they would be a good fit for your academies.

  • 2. Contact your trust relationship manager (TRM)

    Your TRM will help you choose the right trust, and explain what the transfers process involves.

  • 3. Discuss with your board of trustees

    You must get approval from your board of trustees before submitting an application.

  • 4. Gather evidence

    You will need evidence including academic performance, finance data and reasons for joining the trust.

  • 5. Submit your application to the Department for Education

    Submit your academy transfer request, along with your supporting evidence.

  • 6. Wait for a decision

    Your application will be reviewed by an advisory board, before a regional director makes a formal decision.

  • 7. Help complete the transfer

    You'll need to address several things in the delivery phase before the transfer is complete.
